Parkinson's Disease

Parkinson’s disease (PD) is a neurological disorder impacting muscle movement in the body. The brain is the body’s controlling organ. The deficiency and loss of dopamine-producing neurons in brain results in both the motor symptoms, including posture instability, rigidity, tremors, and bradykinesia (slowness of movement) as well as non-motor symptoms, such as depression, anxiety, and sleep disturbances. We see these challenges majorly in people over 60 years. New techniques are needed to diagnose PD. Therefore, less expensive, simplified, and reliable methods should be adapted so that it precisely investigates the presence of PD depending upon their respective data.
